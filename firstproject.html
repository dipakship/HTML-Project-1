Types of Galaxies

There are 3 main categories of galaxies: spiral, elliptical, and irregular that galxies are characterized under based on their visual morphology.

  1. Spiral Galaxies

    • Characterized by their "spiral" shape
    • Account for a large portion of galaxies in our local universe (around 77% of all observed galaxies)
    • Contains actively forming stars
    • Known to be the first type of galaxies
    • ex. Milky Way

  2. Elliptical Galaxies

    • Named after their oblong shape and no overall structure
    • Some may be more spherical while others may be more oval shaped
    • Account for about 10-15% of all galaxies
    • Contains older stars and usually are not actively forming new stars
    • ex. M87

  3. Irregular Galaxies

    • Any galaxy that is not characterized as a spiral or elliptical galaxy, as the name suggests
    • Tend to be small, dwarf galaxies
    • Usually lack any general shape
    • Abundant in the early universe - before spiral and elliptical galaxies were able to form
    • "Look back in time"
    • ex. Small and Large Magellanic clouds
